# CREATIVE-RESET.md
### Santa Barbara Mariachi Festival — Direction v2
*Internal concept. Not authorized, not affiliated, not published. v1 archived at `/archive/prototype-v1-rejected/`.*

---

## 1. The one-line verdict

v1 wasn't broken — it was **ordinary**. It solved the wrong problem beautifully: it built a *concert campaign page* when the job is a *permanent cultural institution's website*. Everything below is about correcting the premise, not polishing the execution.

## 2. Why v1 failed

**2.1 It was a concert, not an institution.**
v1's spine was hero → lineup → seat tiers → CTA. That is an AXS campaign. But the Santa Barbara Mariachi Festival is the *permanent* thing; August 8 is *one edition* of it. The artists change, the lineup changes, the inventory changes — the institution remains. v1 would be stale on August 9. A website that expires the day after its only event is not a website for a 29-year institution; it's a flyer. The correct model is **a permanent cultural platform with a current annual edition nested inside it.**

**2.2 Everything was a box.**
v1 reached for a card, tile, chip, or bordered module for every idea: ticket cards, fact cards, quote cards, sponsor chips, artist tiles. This is dashboard grammar — SaaS grammar — and it is the single biggest reason the page read as cheap. Boxes flatten hierarchy (everything gets the same rectangular weight), they kill pacing (the eye bounces between equal containers), and they signal "template." **A story does not need a card. An artist does not need a tile. A recipient does not need a box. A sponsor does not need a chip.**

**2.3 The palette was a costume.**
Dark navy + brass/gold + red/blue accents is the reflex move for "premium event," and it reads as exactly that: a *theme* applied on top, not a system derived from the subject. Gold-on-black is a luxury cliché; the yellow was loud; the accents drifted toward flag colors. It did not look sophisticated, restrained, or culturally intelligent. It looked *themed.*

**2.4 Dark was mistaken for cinematic.**
A dark background, a big hero, gradients, and hover animations are not cinema. They're a stage set with the lights off. **Cinematic is a property of time and authorship, not of darkness** — pacing, framing, sequence, anticipation, silence, reveal, scale, human detail, light and shadow. v1 had none of that direction. It presented everything at once, at the same volume, inside the same rectangles.

**2.5 No authorship.**
Nothing in v1 decided *what you see first, what you're not shown yet, when information arrives, when the page goes quiet, when it surges.* A visitor never felt a hand guiding them. That absence is what separates a template from a designed experience.

## 3. What the reset keeps

The **research dossier stands** — facts, audit, audience architecture, conversion logic, scholarship truth, bilingual requirements, technical/CMS/analytics/SEO/accessibility plans. That work is sound and is preserved. What changes is the **creative system**: concept, architecture (institution vs. edition), composition, palette, typography, motion, and the emotional sequence.

One correction carried forward as *working client data* (not relitigated): the **2026 lineup — Majo Aguilar, Joss Favela, Mariachi Los Camperos, + more to confirm** — is treated as replaceable annual data. The data model is built so any year's lineup drops in and out without touching the institution's identity. (Where public sources and client-supplied lineup differ, that's flagged once in TOMORROW-REVIEW as a confirmation item, not re-argued.)

## 4. New creative principles

1. **Institution first, edition second.** The homepage represents the permanent festival. The 2026 concert is its current chapter and lives — with full conversion firepower — on its own `/2026` page. The site makes sense in September.
2. **Composition over components.** Full-bleed media, large editorial type, negative space, asymmetry, layered and cropped imagery, horizontal movement where it earns it. Borders and containers are the exception, never the default. Hierarchy comes from **scale, space, and sequence**, not from boxes.
3. **The subject is the richness; the UI is quiet.** Mariachi — the fabric, the brass, the hands, the grito, three generations — carries the emotion and the color. The interface recedes to near-silence so the culture can be loud.
4. **Light, paper, ink — not dark-and-gold.** A restrained editorial/archival palette (warm paper, newsprint greys, carbon ink, silver) with **one** controlled cultural accent used sparingly. Photography carries color; the system does not.
5. **Cinematic = directed time.** Decide the order of revelation. Let sections breathe and go silent, then surge. Motion serves narrative or it doesn't ship — no animation to compensate for weak composition.
6. **Continuity is the concept.** The organizing idea is that *the music is carried forward* — across generations, from stage to student and back. This is what makes the site permanent, makes scholarships central rather than peripheral, and turns "the lineup changes every year" from a problem into the whole point.
7. **Bilingual as a peer.** Spanish is an authored, first-class parallel experience — structurally, not cosmetically.
8. **Restraint is the benchmark.** The reference is the *level of care* in premium editorial and cultural-institution work (the discipline behind Penrose/Valdovieri-tier restraint) — applied to a Mexican-American cultural subject, warm not cold, never erasing the culture, never cosplaying fashion.

## 5. How you'll know v2 is right

- Cover the August 8 concert with your hand: does the site still have a reason to exist? (Must be **yes**.)
- Count the visible rectangles above the fold. (Should be near **zero**.)
- Screenshot any section in greyscale: does hierarchy survive on **type and space** alone? (Must be **yes**.)
- Does it feel like *a different creative team* made it? (The only acceptable answer is yes.)
