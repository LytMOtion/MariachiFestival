# CONCEPT-TERRITORIES.md
### Five conceptual foundations for the permanent website
*Each is a different intellectual and emotional premise — not a restyle of the same idea. For each: core idea · why it belongs to THIS festival · homepage structure · motion · typography · how it survives after Aug 8 · where the annual concert fits.*

---

## Territory 1 — "El son que no termina" / The song that doesn't end
**Core idea.** Mariachi is an *oral, generational* tradition — a song passed mouth to mouth, hand to hand, for centuries. The festival is one continuous song carried across 29 years; each annual concert is the current verse. The scholarship is how the song is literally carried forward: tonight's audience funds tomorrow's musicians, teachers, and parents who will bring the next generation.
**Why it belongs here.** It is *true to mariachi's actual nature* (an inherited, living form), true to the org's actual mechanism (concert → scholarship → students → future), and it is the one idea that is permanent by definition — the song predates and outlives any lineup.
**Homepage structure.** A continuum: opening statement of the song → "this year's verse" (2026) → the music itself → this year's voices (artists) → the reason (scholarship as carrying-forward) → the long song (legacy 1996→now) → the people who carry it (stories) → return to the current verse (CTA).
**Motion.** Continuity motion — long, unbroken vertical flow; sound-inspired reveals (a line of type "sustains" then resolves); archive dissolving into present as one image "becomes" another. Nothing snaps; everything carries.
**Typography.** A characterful high-contrast serif for the "song" (lyrical, human, old-world) against a quiet grotesque for facts — the lyric vs. the liner notes.
**Survives Aug 8?** Completely. The song is the permanent subject; the concert is a verse. In September the site is "between verses," pointing to legacy, stories, scholarships, and next year.
**Where the concert fits.** As *this year's verse* — prominent, dated, ticketed, but explicitly one measure in a longer piece.

## Territory 2 — "The handoff" / De mano en mano
**Core idea.** Tradition as a *relay* between people: performer → listener → student → performer. The festival is the moment the baton is passed. Scholarships are the literal handoff — the older generation in the seats handing opportunity to the younger one on its way to college and, often, back to the music.
**Why it belongs here.** Centers *people*, not abstraction; dramatizes the scholarship as the emotional core (the hand reaching back). Multigenerational audience is the lived truth of these seats.
**Homepage structure.** Paired revelations — each section shows a "giver" and a "receiver": a maestro and a student; a parent and a child; 1996 and 2026; a donor and a recipient. The concert is the gathering where the handoff happens.
**Motion.** Passing motion — elements enter from one side and are "received" by the next section; portraits face each other across the fold.
**Typography.** Warm humanist pairing; italics used as "voice," roman as "record."
**Survives Aug 8?** Yes — the handoff is year-round (applications, awards, alumni). Slightly more people-dependent on fresh recipient content.
**Where the concert fits.** As the annual ceremony of the handoff.

## Territory 3 — "The long echo" / Se oye a lo lejos
**Core idea.** Every note played at the Bowl *echoes forward* — into a student's degree, a career, a family, the next festival. Time and resonance are the organizing metaphors: what happens on one night is heard for decades.
**Why it belongs here.** Uniquely fuses the archive (29 years of sound) with impact (scholarship outcomes) under one idea; very cinematic (echo = delay, reverb, memory).
**Homepage structure.** Cause and long-effect — a present moment, then its echoes fanning out across years and lives; the legacy and stories are "the echo" of past concerts.
**Motion.** Echo/reverb motion — a reveal repeats faintly, delayed; scroll feels like sound decaying across time; the year-rail behaves like a waveform.
**Typography.** High-contrast display that can be set very large and "reverberate" (repeated ghosted headline), with a clean text face.
**Survives Aug 8?** Strongly — after the concert, the site *is* the echo. Risk: the metaphor is subtle and could read as arty if under-explained.
**Where the concert fits.** As the source of the echo — the strike that resonates.

## Territory 4 — "Under the oaks" / Bajo los robles
**Core idea.** Rootedness in *place and ritual*: the Santa Barbara Bowl's sandstone terraces and oak-lined hillside, and the annual return on Fiesta Saturday. The festival as a sacred yearly gathering at a specific, beloved place.
**Why it belongs here.** The Bowl is genuinely singular and locally loved; Fiesta timing is a real civic ritual; "hometown tradition" is a verified frame in the press. Very ownable imagery (light, stone, oaks, dusk).
**Homepage structure.** A sense of arrival and place — approach the venue, enter, the night unfolds, the community gathers, and it returns each year. Legacy = "how many times we've gathered here."
**Motion.** Arrival motion — golden-hour light shifts, gates open, dusk falls across the scroll; slow and atmospheric.
**Typography.** Editorial serif with a Californian, sun-worn warmth; place-name typography.
**Survives Aug 8?** Moderately — place is permanent, but "the gathering" is event-anchored; off-season the site leans on legacy/place. Risk: drifts toward *tourism/venue* branding rather than the institution's mission.
**Where the concert fits.** As the annual gathering itself — central, but risks making the venue the hero over the mission.

## Territory 5 — "El grito" / The cry at the center
**Core idea.** The single, raw human cry at the heart of mariachi — the *grito* — as the emotional nucleus: breath, voice, feeling made audible. The most visceral, present-tense premise.
**Why it belongs here.** It's the most emotionally immediate, most unmistakably mariachi idea; hard for any competitor to out-feel.
**Homepage structure.** Emotion-forward — build to and release the grito; the concert is where the cry goes up together; scholarships are "giving the next voice its chance."
**Motion.** Breath motion — inhale/hold/release pacing; big quiet then sudden scale.
**Typography.** Expressive, large, almost vocal display type; dramatic scale contrast.
**Survives Aug 8?** Weakest of the five — emotion is present-tense and event-shaped; harder to sustain a *permanent* institutional home on a single cry. Best as a *campaign* energy, not the permanent spine.
**Where the concert fits.** As the moment the grito is released — excellent for the 2026 page, thinner as the year-round identity.

## Selection

**Primary concept: Territory 1 — "El son que no termina / The song that doesn't end,"** with **Territory 2 (The handoff)** adopted as its *scholarship mechanism* and **Territory 4 (Under the oaks)** supplying the *place imagery* for the 2026 edition.

**Why.**
- **It is permanent by construction.** The song is the subject; the lineup is a verse. This is the only premise that fully solves Problem 1 (institution vs. concert) at the level of *idea*, not just architecture.
- **It makes scholarships structural, not sentimental.** "Carrying the song forward" *is* the scholarship — students literally become the tradition's future. Mission stops being a footer and becomes the plot.
- **It turns the annual-change problem into the point.** Every year a new verse; the continuity is the brand. The data model (replaceable edition) and the concept agree.
- **It's culturally honest.** Mariachi *is* an inherited, oral, generational form. This is the culture's own truth, not a decorative metaphor imposed on it.
- **It's directable.** "The song that doesn't end" gives clear authorship cues: where to be quiet (between verses), where to surge (the current verse), when history enters (the long song), when the mission is revealed (carried forward).

**Handoff (T2)** is folded in because it gives the scholarship section its human faces and its emotional reach-back; **Under the oaks (T4)** is reserved for the 2026 page and the venue/visit material, where place and ritual do real conversion work without hijacking the institution's identity.

**Working brand-idea territory (not locked copy):** *The song that doesn't end.* · *El son que no termina.* · *One night. A song that outlasts it.* · *The music is carried forward.* Final line to be selected with the client; the structure does not depend on the exact words.

**Rejected as primary (kept as accents):** T3 (Echo) is beautiful but too oblique to lead; T5 (Grito) is the right *energy* for the 2026 campaign page but too present-tense to be the permanent spine; T4 alone risks tourism branding.
