# MISSING-MEDIA.md
### Gaps in the current concept media, and what to shoot to replace it
*The 14 AI assets cover the full arc, but two structural gaps limit the design. All AI media is placeholder and must be replaced with real, licensed festival photography/footage regardless.*

## Gap 1 — No portrait-orientation assets (all sources are 16:9 landscape)
- **Why it matters:** mobile scenes crop a wide frame into a tall viewport. It works for single-subject shots (hero trumpet, student, venue), but the **family / "three generations" scene** loses the grandfather and child on a 390px crop — the "three generations" idea weakens exactly where most traffic is (mobile).
- **Need:** a **vertical (4:5 or 9:16) family/multigenerational** frame, subjects grouped centrally so a tall crop keeps all three generations.
- **Also ideal in portrait:** one artist portrait and one recipient portrait (for the overlap/plane treatments).

## Gap 2 — Real, licensed, non-AI assets for anything that implies a real person, artist, or the venue
Priority order (highest homepage impact first):

1. **Hero footage** — real owned trumpet close-up (or equivalent "before the note") at the Bowl. 6–10s, quiet controlled motion, room for type lower-left. 16:9 **and** a 9:16 cut.
2. **Confirmed-artist photography** — real licensed images of the actual 2026 lineup. Until then, scene 05 shows **names only**; the AI portraits are labeled "concept image — not the artist" and must be swapped before any publication.
3. **Real scholarship-recipient portrait + short testimonial** (consented) — replaces the AI student; this is the emotional hinge of the page.
4. **Real Santa Barbara Bowl** exterior/blue-hour + on-stage footage — the current venue/finale frames are AI approximations, **not** the actual Bowl, and cannot be presented as documentary.
5. **Graded archival ↔ present pair** for the legacy scene — a real 1990s/2000s frame and a recent one, so the mono→color transition is true history, not a synthetic double-exposure.
6. **Performance footage at the Bowl** — real release/finale energy to replace the AI performance clips.

## Gap 3 — Documentary scholarship-student image (A3 requirement)
The current placeholder (`scholarships/student-future.jpg`) reads as **AI stock / university-brochure** — a centered student smiling at camera holding books. Keep it only as a temporary layout placeholder.
- **Needed:** documentary-style Latino student, **natural campus environment**, candid or lightly directed, **premium editorial photography**, strong California light, **no stock-photo smile**, **no books-as-prop cliché**. Landscape **and** portrait crops.
- **Also needed:** a real *performance* image of a female singer (microphone / stage light / singing gesture / audience relationship) — the current female concept image reads as a beauty/jewelry campaign, not a live performer (see MEDIA-MAP addendum).

## Camera/spec notes for the shoot
- Shoot **both** 16:9 and 9:16 (or 4:5) of every hero-candidate moment so no scene depends on a crop that destroys composition.
- Keep intentional **negative space** in hero/venue frames for type.
- Preserve the concept's **color arc** (intimate red/cobalt → daylight family/scholarship → blue-hour venue → red/cobalt finale) when grading.
- Get **releases** for all identifiable people, especially minors and recipients.

*What we did NOT do: compensate for these gaps with gradients, glyphs, or brown placeholders. Where an asset is missing or weak, the layout is built to accept the real file without redesign (see code comments `REPLACE:` and MEDIA-MAP.md).*
