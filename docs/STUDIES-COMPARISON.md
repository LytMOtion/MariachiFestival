## Read on each
- **A — Refined Editorial.** The most classically elegant and the safest "premium." Cormorant reads warm and timeless. **Risk:** it's the most *expected* of the three, and Cormorant is comparatively common; it edges toward the "luxury serif + warm paper" territory you flagged. Beautiful, but least distinctive.
- **B — Contemporary Institution.** The calmest and most understated. The cobalt accent is a smart, non-obvious choice that separates the UI from the red imagery and signals "serious cultural institution" without warmth-cliché. **Risk:** all-grotesque can feel a touch cool/corporate for a subject this human; least "alive."
- **C — Cultural Modernism.** The most distinctive and the most *alive while still elegant*. Instrument Serif has real character (high contrast, editorial flair) that reads culturally rich without any cliché; vermilion is warm and energetic yet controlled, with bougainvillea held in reserve. **Risk:** a distinctive display face demands disciplined spacing and restraint everywhere else — it's the highest-skill option to execute well (but that's exactly the bar you're asking for).

## Recommendation
**Lead with C — Cultural Modernism.** It best satisfies the brief's actual words — *distinctive display + restrained sans; alive; elegant; confident; culturally intelligent; excellent Spanish* — and it's the one most likely to make an organizer feel their festival was reimagined by a serious studio. It is vibrant without being a poster, and refined without being cold or generic.

**Strong alternative: B**, if you'd prefer a fully sans, maximally understated "museum" register with the longest visual shelf life.

**A** is the fallback if you want the most conventional, safest elegance — I'd rank it third for *this* brief precisely because it's the least distinctive.

**Refinement I'd apply to the chosen direction when building:** reserve the display face for the wordmark, hero, and section titles only; Hanken for everything functional; keep the accent to ~1–2 uses per screen (CTA + one label), with bougainvillea/marigold truly rare; hold the generous margins and hairline detailing shown here.
